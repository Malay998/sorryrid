What I Should've Said
instead of letting frustration speak for me
◆

I've replayed that conversation in my head more times than I can count.
And honestly?
There were a thousand better ways I could've handled it.

Maybe I was frustrated.
Maybe I felt unheard.
Maybe I was tired, overthinking, irritated, or emotionally overwhelmed.
But none of that changes one simple thing:
I still hurt you with the way I spoke.

I think somewhere in the middle of trying to explain myself,
I stopped paying attention to how my words would actually feel to you.
And that's on me.

Some of the things I said sounded cold.
Some sounded dismissive.
Some probably made you feel like your emotions were a burden instead of something safe to express.
And that's the last thing I ever wanted you to feel around me.

The truth is...
I wasn't angry because I hated talking to you.
I was angry because I care too much sometimes and don't always know how to express it correctly.
And instead of communicating calmly, I reacted emotionally.

You didn't deserve sarcasm.
You didn't deserve attitude.
And you definitely didn't deserve to feel like I was trying to "win" instead of understand.

"I should've spoken softer.
Especially to someone I love this much."
there's more i need to tell you
written with guilt, love & a promise to do better
