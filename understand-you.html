The Parts You
Never Show
the things i noticed even when you thought i didn't
01

You pretend you're okay too often

I notice the way you say "it's fine" when it clearly isn't. The way your replies get shorter when something hurts you. The way sarcasm becomes easier than admitting you're upset.

And sometimes I react to the tone instead of realizing there's an actual feeling hiding underneath it.

02

You care more than you admit

You act nonchalant. You act like "do whatever." You act like you don't care. But honestly? People who truly don't care don't get hurt that deeply in the first place.

And I think somewhere underneath all the attitude and sarcasm... there's just someone who wanted to feel important.

03

I misunderstood your reactions

Instead of asking, "why did that hurt you?" I started defending myself immediately. And that turned the conversation into me versus you, instead of us versus the problem.

That's something I genuinely need to change.

04

You were never a burden

Not your calls. Not your texts. Not your emotions. Not even the moments where we argued.

I know some of my words probably made it feel that way, and I hate that they did. Because if anything, you became one of the biggest parts of my everyday life.

Sometimes I act logical when I should just be gentle. Sometimes I explain instead of listening. Sometimes I focus so much on whether I'm being understood, that I forget the other person needs understanding too.

And I think that's what happened here.

You weren't trying to fight me. You weren't trying to ruin my mood. You just wanted emotional reassurance in your own way, and I answered with frustration instead of softness.

I know I can be intense sometimes. I know my words become sharper when I'm overwhelmed. But none of that changes how much you matter to me.

◆

Even after the argument, even after the dry replies, even after the sarcasm, even after pretending not to care...

I still sat there thinking about you.

Loving someone properly also means learning how they hurt.
